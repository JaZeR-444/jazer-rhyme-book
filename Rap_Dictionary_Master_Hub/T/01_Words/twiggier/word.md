# WORD: Twiggier

## Meaning (plain):
The comparative form of twiggy; more slender, fragile, or covered with more twigs/branches.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for a rival whose street cred is becoming "thinner" and more fragile. Used to describe someone who is losing their "weight" and becoming easier to snap like a twig. A status of increasing weakness and lack of substance.

## Syllables:
3 (twig-gi-er)

## Part of speech:
adjective

## Synonyms:
thinner, more-fragile, slender, weak, branchy

## Antonyms:
thicker, sturdier, heavier, robust

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: figure, trigger, vigor / SLANT: bigger, liver, shiver / MULTI: getting twiggier, losing the vigor, watch the trigger

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Your reputation is getting twiggier, easier to snap.
2. Fragile with the rhythm, twiggier in the trap.
3. No more weight here, twiggier while I cap.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Increasing "thinness"/weakness of a rival
2. Snap-able/fragile status
3. Twiggier vs. Heavier contrast

## Tags:
nature, common, unique, insult, weakness

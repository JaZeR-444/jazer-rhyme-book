# WORD: Zero-sum

## Meaning (plain):
A situation in which one person's gain is equivalent to another's loss, resulting in a net change of zero.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing a "win-lose" game where there's no room for everyone; if I win, you lose, and I'm coming for it all.

## Syllables:
3 (ze-ro-sum)

## Part of speech:
Adjective/Noun

## Synonyms:
competitive, balanced, win-lose, fixed-pie

## Antonyms:
win-win, collaborative

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: come, drum, gum / SLANT: run, sun / MULTI: Zero-sum game, never be the same, chasing the fame

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. This industry is Zero-sum, I'm taking your spot.
2. No participation trophies, it's Zero-sum on the block.
3. Every gain I make is your loss, pure Zero-sum.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Win-Lose" nature of competition
2. Taking the "whole pie" (no net gain for others)
3. Ruthless business/street logic

## Tags:
economics, logic, competition, strategy

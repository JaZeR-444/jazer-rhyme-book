# WORD: Kaitaka

## Meaning (plain):
A traditional Māori cloak or cape made from the finest flax fiber, often featuring an intricate, geometric border (taniko). Considered highly prestigious.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for wearing a cloak of prestige and cultural mastery. Represents being "draped" in excellence and fine craftsmanship that is rooted in indigenous pride.

## Syllables:
3 (kai-ta-ka)

## Part of speech:
noun

## Synonyms:
cloak, mantle, fine-flax, prestigious-dress

## Antonyms:
N/A

1. Draped in the kaitaka, a master of the land.
2. Geometric borders, kaitaka in the hand.
3. Cultural prestige, kaitaka while I lead.

## 3 punchline angles:

3. Indigenous/Māori pride and strength

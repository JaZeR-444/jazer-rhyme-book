# WORD: Bookways

## Meaning (plain):
1. In the manner or style of a book. 2. Relating to "Bookways: A Quarterly for the Book Arts," which focused on fine printing and typography.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for a literary, sophisticated approach to the game. Represents someone who is "by the book" in terms of quality but creative in their "typography" (the way they present their words). A well-documented, artful style.

## Syllables:
2 (book-ways)

## Part of speech:
adjective / adverb

## Synonyms:
literary, scholarly, documented, artful, typographic

## Antonyms:
oral, messy, unpolished, illiterate

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: days, shows, pays / SLANT: phase, blaze, glaze / MULTI: bookways style, documented file, stay for a while

2. Documented greatness, keeping it bookways and smooth.
3. Every verse is a chapter, bookways for the truth.

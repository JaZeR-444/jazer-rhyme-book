# WORD: Zipline

## Meaning (plain):
A pulley suspended on a cable, mounted on a slope, used for fast gravity-propelled travel.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing a "fast track" to the bottom (the drop) or moving smoothly and quickly through high-altitude danger.

## Syllables:
2 (zip-line)

## Part of speech:
Noun/Verb

## Synonyms:
cable, wire, fast track, glide

## Antonyms:
climb, crawl

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: fine, mine, shine, sign / SLANT: design, align / MULTI: Zipline drop, never gonna stop, right to the top

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Gliding over the danger on a Zipline.
2. Fast track to the loot, I'm on the Zipline.
3. High altitude moves, follow the Zipline.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Fast Track" (Speed/Efficiency)
2. High Altitude/Danger (Bravery)
3. Gravity-propelled momentum

## Tags:
movement, direction, speed, height

# WORD: Jambeau

## Meaning (plain):
A piece of medieval armor (a greave) designed to protect the leg below the knee.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for being "protected from the ground up." Represents having the necessary "armor" to navigate the lower levels of the struggle without getting hurt. A solid, defensive base.

## Syllables:
2 (jam-beau)

## Part of speech:
noun

## Synonyms:
greave, armor, protection, shin-guard

## Antonyms:
exposed, vulnerable, barefoot

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: show, go, slow / SLANT: jumbo, combo, mambo / MULTI: jambeau plate, safe in the state, never late

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Protected in the struggle, jambeau on the leg.
2. Armored from the bottom, no reason to beg.
3. Every step is guarded, jambeau in the ring.

## 3 punchline angles:

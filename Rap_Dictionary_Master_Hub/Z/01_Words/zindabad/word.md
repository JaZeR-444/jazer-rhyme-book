# WORD: Zindabad

## Meaning (plain):
An interjection of Persian origin (used in Urdu/Hindi) meaning "Long Live"; used as a cheer or political slogan of support.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing ultimate loyalty and "long life" to the crew or the movement; a shout of eternal support.

## Syllables:
3 (zin-da-bad)

## Part of speech:
Interjection

## Synonyms:
long live, hurrah, hail, support

## Antonyms:
murdabad (death to)

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: pad, mad, dad, glad / SLANT: nomad, comrade / MULTI: Zindabad life, through the fire and strife, sharp like a knife

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Long live the movement, holla Zindabad.
2. Loyalty is deep, screaming out Zindabad.
3. Eternal support, call the crew Zindabad.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Long Live" (Immortality/Legacy)
2. Political/Social slogan power
3. "Zindabad/Mad" (Passionate support)

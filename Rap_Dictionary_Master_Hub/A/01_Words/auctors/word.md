# WORD: Auctors

## Meaning (plain):
The plural of "auctor," meaning an author, source, or originator of a title, right, or work. In Roman law, it refers to a seller or one who warrants a title.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for the original creators or the "source" of the style. Represents those who own the "title" to the flow and act as the ultimate authority on the craft.

## Syllables:
2 (auc-tors)

## Part of speech:
noun (plural)

## Synonyms:
authors, originators, sources, principals

## Antonyms:
copies, followers, plagiarists

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: doors, stars, wars / SLANT: factors, actors, tractors / MULTI: auctors of the game, original name, never the same

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. We the auctors of the flow, originating the sound.
2. Original sources, auctors on the ground.
3. Owning the title, auctors when we're found.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Originality/source status
2. Ownership of a style or "title"
3. Authority over the creative process

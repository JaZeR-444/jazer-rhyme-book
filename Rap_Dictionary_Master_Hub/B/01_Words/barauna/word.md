# WORD: Barauna

## Meaning (plain):
1. A municipality in Brazil, meaning "place of the reeds" in Tupi. 2. A term for quebracho wood, known for its extreme hardness.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for being "hard as wood" or having an unbreakable foundation. Represents someone who is rooted in their place (reeds) but has the toughness of quebracho wood.

## Syllables:
3 (ba-rau-na)

## Part of speech:
noun

## Synonyms:
quebracho, hardwood, reed-place, foundation

## Antonyms:
softwood, fragile, temporary

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: trauma, sauna, fauna / SLANT: town, down, crown / MULTI: barauna wood, understood, through the hood

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Hard like barauna, I never break or bend.
2. Rooted in the place of reeds, barauna to the end.
3. Unbreakable spirit, barauna that I send.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Extreme hardness/toughness (quebracho wood)
2. Being rooted in a specific "place" (Mindanao/Brazil link)
3. Unbreakable foundation

nature, unique, geography, strength

# WORD: Glimpse

## Meaning (plain):
A brief or quick view or look; to see something or someone for a very short time.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for a "sneak peek" at greatness or for something that moves so fast the crowd only sees a flash of it. Represents a high-speed, elusive talent that leaves people wanting more.

## Syllables:
1 (glimpse)

## Part of speech:
noun / verb

## Synonyms:
peek, flash, glance, sight, view

## Antonyms:
stare, gaze, scrutinize, overlook

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: prince, since, mince / SLANT: tints, hints, prints / MULTI: catch a glimpse, move like a prince, ever since

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. They only caught a glimpse, I move with the speed of light.
2. Just a flash, a glimpse of the power in the night.
3. Elusive with the flow, a glimpse is all they know.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Moving too fast to be caught
2. Brief/elusive greatness
3. Leaving the crowd wanting more (sneak peek)

## Tags:
movement, status, common, unique

# WORD: Zimmi

## Meaning (plain):
A historical term (dhimmi) for non-Muslims living in an Islamic state, granted protection and certain rights in exchange for a tax (jizya).

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing a protected but "taxed" status; being an outsider who still has a place in the community, or paying the "dues" for safety.

## Syllables:
2 (zim-mi)

## Part of speech:
Noun

## Synonyms:
dhimmi, protected person, outsider

## Antonyms:
None

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: me, free, knee, tree / SLANT: semi, heavy / MULTI: Zimmi life, through the strife, sharp like a knife

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Paid the dues, now I'm living as a Zimmi.
2. Protected in the town, respect the Zimmi.
3. Outsider looking in, spirit of a Zimmi.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Paying Dues" (Jizya tax metaphor)
2. Protected Status (Safety within a culture)
3. Outsider/Insider dynamics

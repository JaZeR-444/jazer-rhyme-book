# WORD: Gambits

## Meaning (plain):
Devices, actions, or opening remarks, typically involving a degree of risk, that are calculated to gain an advantage.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for risky opening moves or calculated plays intended to take over the game. Represents a high-stakes strategy where you sacrifice something small to win the whole board.

## Syllables:
2 (gam-bits)

## Part of speech:
noun (plural)

## Synonyms:
ploys, tactics, stratagems, opening-moves, maneuvers

## Antonyms:
blunders, mistakes, reactions

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: hits, sits, pits / SLANT: admits, commits, splits / MULTI: risky gambits, win the pits, handle the hits

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Opening with gambits, high stakes in the play.
2. Calculated maneuvers, gambits all day.
3. Sacrificed the pawn, gambits in the fray.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Risky opening moves
2. Sacrifice for long-term gain
3. Strategic/chess-like brilliance

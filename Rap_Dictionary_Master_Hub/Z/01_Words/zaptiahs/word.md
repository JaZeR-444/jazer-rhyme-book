# WORD: Zaptiahs

## Meaning (plain):
Plural of zaptiah (or zaptieh); Turkish police officers, particularly from the Ottoman era.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing a group of enforcers, the "law" coming for the game, or a disciplined squad of patrols.

## Syllables:
3 (zap-ti-ahs)

## Part of speech:
Noun (Plural)

## Synonyms:
police, officers, guards, enforcers

## Antonyms:
outlaws, criminals, rebels

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: stars, cars, bars / SLANT: fires, liars / MULTI: Zaptiahs on the prowl, hear 'em growl, throw in the towel

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Squad full of enforcers, move like Zaptiahs.
2. Ottoman spirit, we the street Zaptiahs.
3. Patrol the block, no room for Zaptiahs.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Squad/Enforcer collective imagery
2. Historical Turkish/Ottoman patrol
3. "The Law" coming to shut it down

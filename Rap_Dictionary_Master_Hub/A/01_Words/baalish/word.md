# WORD: Baalish

## Meaning (plain):
1. Pertaining to Baal, the ancient Canaanite deity, or the practice of Baalism (worship of false idols). 2. (Persian) A pillow or cushion.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for false idolatry or for being "soft" like a cushion. Used to describe someone who is worshipping the wrong things or who has lost their edge and become too comfortable.

## Syllables:
2 (baal-ish)

## Part of speech:
adjective

## Synonyms:
idolatrous, cushioned, soft, false, comfortable

## Antonyms:
genuine, hard, ascetic, true

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: fish, dish, wish / SLANT: polish, abolish, demolish / MULTI: baalish state, false weight, cushioned in the gate

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Worshipping the money, that's a baalish state of mind.
2. Too comfortable and soft, baalish in the grind.
3. Don't fall for the idols, that baalish design.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. False idols/idolatry
2. "Soft" vs. "Hard" (cushion metaphor)
3. Getting too comfortable/losing the edge

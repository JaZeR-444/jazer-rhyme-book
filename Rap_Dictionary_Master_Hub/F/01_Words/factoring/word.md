# WORD: Factoring

## Meaning (plain):
1. (Finance) A transaction where a business sells its accounts receivable to a third party at a discount for immediate cash. 2. (Math) The process of writing a number or expression as a product of factors.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for cashing in on your future success or breaking down a complex situation into simple parts. Represents calculating the "factors" of a move and extracting the value immediately.

## Syllables:
3 (fac-tor-ing)

## Part of speech:
noun / verb

## Synonyms:
liquidating, calculating, dividing, extracting, assessing

## Antonyms:
multiplying, combining, hoarding

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: ring, sing, bring / SLANT: scattering, battering, flattering / MULTI: factoring the cost, counting the loss, never be tossed

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Factoring the risk before I make the call.
2. Cashing in early, factoring it all.
3. Complex equation, factoring the fall.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Calculating variables of success
2. Liquidating value for immediate gain
3. Breaking down a complex rival

# WORD: Zionward

## Meaning (plain):
Moving or directed toward Zion; often used figuratively to mean toward heaven or the goal of salvation.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Always aiming for the "promised land" of success; the upward journey toward the ultimate peak.

## Syllables:
3 (zi-on-ward)

## Part of speech:
Adverb/Adjective

## Synonyms:
heavenward, upward, topward

## Antonyms:
downward, babylonward

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: guard, hard, card, yard / SLANT: iron, lion / MULTI: Zionward bound, never looking down, king of the town

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Moving Zionward, I'm reaching for the crown.
2. My trajectory is Zionward, never coming down.
3. Aim the vision Zionward, past the clouds.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Salvation/Success" (Aiming for the goal)
2. Upward movement vs the "downward" spiral
3. Spiritual trajectory

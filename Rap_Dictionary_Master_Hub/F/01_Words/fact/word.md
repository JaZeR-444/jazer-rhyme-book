# WORD: Fact

## Meaning (plain):
A thing that is known or proved to be true; a true datum about a circumstance.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
The absolute truth of the streets. It's the opposite of "cap" (lying). When something is a fact, it's undeniable, documented, and respected as the baseline of reality.

## Syllables:
1 (fact)

## Part of speech:
noun

## Synonyms:
truth, reality, datum, certainty, verity

## Antonyms:
fiction, lie, cap, myth, falsehood

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: act, track, packed / SLANT: impact, exact, distract / MULTI: state of fact, keep it tracked, never slack

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Everything I speak is an undeniable fact.
2. No cap here, just the facts on the track.
3. Proven by the history, grounded in the fact.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Real vs. Cap" (Fact vs. Lie)
2. Undeniable street truth
3. Documented/proven reality

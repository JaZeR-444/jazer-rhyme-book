# WORD: Lark

## Meaning (plain):
1. A small songbird known for its singing. 2. (Informal) A carefree adventure, frolic, or something done for fun or mischief.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for someone who makes the "song" of the street look easy and carefree. Represents a playful, melodic flow that doesn't take itself too seriously but still carries a beautiful "sing-song" weight.

## Syllables:
1 (lark)

## Part of speech:
noun / verb

## Synonyms:
songbird, frolic, adventure, mischief, singer

## Antonyms:
seriousness, tragedy, labor

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: dark, spark, mark / SLANT: heart, part, start / MULTI: street lark, leave a mark, in the dark

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Singin' through the struggle like a common lark.
2. Carefree adventure, leaving my lark mark.
3. Mischief in the rhythm, lark while I live 'em.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Playful/melodic delivery
2. Making the struggle look "fun" (frolic)
3. Being the "songbird" of the streets

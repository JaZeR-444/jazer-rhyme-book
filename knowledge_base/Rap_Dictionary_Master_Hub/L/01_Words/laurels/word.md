# WORD: Laurels

## Meaning (plain):
Honor, distinction, or fame achieved through great accomplishment; historically, a wreath made of laurel foliage worn as a symbol of victory.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for the "crown of victory" and the honors earned through the struggle. Represents reaching a level of success where you are recognized as a champion. "Resting on your laurels" means stopping the hustle because you're satisfied with past wins.

## Syllables:
2 (lau-rels)

## Part of speech:
noun (plural)

## Synonyms:
honors, distinction, fame, victory-wreath, crown

## Antonyms:
disgrace, failure, obscurity

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: bells, tells, swells / SLANT: morals, corals, orals / MULTI: earn the laurels, moral quarrels, floral patterns

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. I earned the laurels, the victory is mine.
2. Resting on my laurels while I'm drinking on the wine.
3. Crown made of laurels, purely divine.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Achieving victory/symbolic honor
2. "Resting on laurels" (laziness after success)
3. Historical/Roman status symbols

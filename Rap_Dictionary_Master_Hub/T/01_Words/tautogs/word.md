# WORD: Tautogs

## Meaning (plain):
Plural of tautog; a species of wrasse (blackfish) found in the western Atlantic, known for having thick lips and powerful jaws for crushing prey.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for a "crushing" dominance and unshakeable strength. Represents someone who has the "jaws" to break through any defense and the "thick lips" (heavy speech) to command the deep waters of the street. A powerful, bottom-dwelling force that eats the competition.

## Syllables:
2 (tau-togs)

## Part of speech:
noun (plural)

## Synonyms:
blackfish, wrasses, crushers, heavy-hitters

## Antonyms:
prey, minnows, weaklings

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: logs, dogs, frogs / SLANT: catalogs, monologues, dialogues / MULTI: street tautogs, purely for the dogs, heavy logs

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Crushing the fakes like a school of tautogs.
2. Powerful jaws, moving through the fogs.
3. Every move is a bite, tautogs in the night.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Crushing" the competition
2. Heavy/Powerful jaws metaphors
3. Dominating the "deep waters"

# WORD: Addlings

## Meaning (plain):
1. Plural of addling; eggs that will not hatch due to being old or infertile. 2. (Obsolete/Provincial) Earnings or wages. 3. The process of making something 'addled' or confused.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for missed potential (unhatched eggs) or for the confusing 'mental fog' caused by pressure. Can also be used as an obscure term for 'earnings' in a vintage context.

## Syllables:
2 (ad-dlings)

## Part of speech:
noun (plural)

## Synonyms:
earnings, wages, failures, duds

## Antonyms:
successes, offspring, clarity

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: rings, things, kings / SLANT: brings, wings, sings / MULTI: addlings game, golden rings, small things

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Countin' my addlings, the earnings from the street.
2. Left 'em with addlings, eggs that couldn't handle the heat.
3. Mind in the addlings, lost in the rhythm and beat.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Failed potential/unhatched plans
2. Mental confusion/overwhelming pressure
3. Obscure wealth/earnings reference

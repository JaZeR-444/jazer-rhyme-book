# WORD: Aroast

## Meaning (plain):
Likely a variant or misspelling of "roast," meaning to cook food by dry heat or, informally, to subject someone to severe criticism or humorous insults.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for "cooking" an opponent in a battle or on a track. Represents a high-intensity verbal attack that leaves the target "burnt" or exposed.

## Syllables:
2 (a-roast)

## Part of speech:
verb / noun

## Synonyms:
roast, cook, burn, criticize, battle

## Antonyms:
praise, compliment, defend

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: coast, most, ghost / SLANT: roast, boast, toast / MULTI: aroast the crew, nothing new, pay the due

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. I'm coming to aroast the whole scene, leave 'em burnt.
2. No praise here, just an aroast for what you haven't learnt.
3. Every bar a flame, aroast with the intent.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Cooking" the competition
2. Leaving a rival "burnt" (exposed or defeated)
3. High-intensity verbal heat

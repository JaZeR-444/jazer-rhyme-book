## Redaction Policy (Internal Safety Rules)
- Use initials only for private people; never full names.
- Avoid street names, case details, amounts, employer names, and full dates (year-only if needed).
- Convert any conflict into **“what I learned / how I grew.”**
- If uncertain whether something is public-safe, treat it as internal.

### Redaction Ladder (Fast)
1) Remove identifiers (names, places, timelines)
2) Convert to a cinematic scene (sensory, not legalistic)
3) Swap specifics for feelings (pressure, relief, hunger)
4) Add metaphor anchors (beam / road / scoreboard / doorway)
5) Tag: **SAFE_PUBLIC / SAFE_LYRICS / SAFE_SOCIAL**

## Studio Habits & Workflow

## Proof of Craft (How the Songs Get Built)
I build songs like a slow climb: I start with a pocket or a phrase, then I keep carving until the hook feels like a door you can step through. The room is quiet, the screens are low, and the loop runs until the sound stops being noise and becomes signal. When the take lands, I know because the air shifts and the lyric feels lighter, not louder. The mission stays in the center the whole time: make something that feels like home for people who carry a lot and still move forward.

**Process truths**
- The hook is the compass, not the trophy.
- A clean take beats a loud take.
- The first 30 seconds must lift and land.

- Late-night sessions when the room is quiet
- Multiple writing passes for rhythm, clarity, and punch
- Hook-first on some tracks, verse-first on others
- Sound design layers that build a beam-like atmosphere
- Reference playlists to keep the vibe locked
- Car-test bounces to check low end and energy
- Voice memos for bars, hooks, and melody sparks
- Simple mix notes so every revision moves forward

### Workflow Loop (Repeatable)
1) **Spark:** capture the line/scene
2) **Structure:** hook thesis + 8 bar seeds
3) **Build:** arrange the beat around the hook
4) **Polish:** clarity pass + cadence pass
5) **Test:** car test + first 30 seconds check

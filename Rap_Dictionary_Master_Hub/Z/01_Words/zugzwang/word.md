# WORD: Zugzwang

## Meaning (plain):
A situation in chess where a player is forced to make a move, even though any move will result in a disadvantage or loss.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing a "checkmate" state for the competition; forcing the opponent into a position where every move they make is a mistake.

## Syllables:
2 (zug-zwang)

## Part of speech:
Noun

## Synonyms:
deadlock, predicament, bind, forced-move

## Antonyms:
advantage, freedom

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: bang, rang, sang, hang / SLANT: long, strong / MULTI: Zugzwang move, in the groove, nothing left to prove

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. I got 'em trapped, call it Zugzwang.
2. Every move you make is a loss, purely Zugzwang.
3. Checkmate imminent, you caught in the Zugzwang.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Trapped/Forced Move" (Strategic dominance)
2. "Disadvantageous Move" (Inevitability of failure for others)
3. Chess metaphors/Mastery

## Tags:
gaming, strategy, status, power

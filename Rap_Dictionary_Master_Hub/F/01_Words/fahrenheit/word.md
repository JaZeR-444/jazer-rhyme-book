# WORD: Fahrenheit

## Meaning (plain):
A temperature scale used mostly in the US, where water freezes at 32° and boils at 212°.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
The ultimate metaphor for "heat." Represents someone who is boiling hot or bringing an intense, high-temperature energy to the mic. Turning up the Fahrenheit means increasing the pressure.

## Syllables:
3 (fahr-en-heit)

## Part of speech:
noun

## Synonyms:
heat, temperature, boiling-point, pressure

## Antonyms:
Celsius, freezing, cold

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: light, fight, height / SLANT: tonight, bright, right / MULTI: fahrenheit heat, burn up the street, never retreat

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Turning up the fahrenheit, watch the booth melt.
2. High-temperature flow, fahrenheit felt.
3. Boiling at 212, fahrenheit in the belt.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Boiling" intensity (212 degrees)
2. Bringing the "heat" to the scene
3. Increasing pressure/temperature

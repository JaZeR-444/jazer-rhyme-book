# WORD: Zillion

## Meaning (plain):
An informal term for an extremely large, unspecified number; a huge quantity used for exaggeration.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing "infinite" wealth, too many bars to count, or having an unquantifiable impact on the game.

## Syllables:
2 (zil-lion)

## Part of speech:
Noun/Adjective

## Synonyms:
billion, gazillion, infinite, countless, myriad

## Antonyms:
one, zero, few

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: million, billion, stallion, trillion / SLANT: civilian, pavilion / MULTI: Zillion in the bank, never gonna sank, moving like a tank

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. I got a zillion ways to win this fight.
2. Counting up a zillion stars in the night.
3. Too many bars to count, I'm dropping a zillion.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "Uncountable Wealth" (Exaggerated riches)
2. "Infinite Skill" (Too many ways to win)
3. Exaggeration for impact ("Zillion/Million")

## Tags:
math, logic, quantity, absolute

# WORD: Gloat

## Meaning (plain):
To observe or think about something with triumphant and often malicious satisfaction, especially over someone else's misfortune or failure.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
The act of taking a "victory lap" and letting the competition know they lost. Represents the satisfaction of reaching the top while others fall, and not being humble about the win.

## Syllables:
1 (gloat)

## Part of speech:
verb

## Synonyms:
triumph, celebrate, relish, brag, exult

## Antonyms:
commiserate, sympathize, mourn

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: boat, coat, throat / SLANT: wrote, note, vote / MULTI: raise the float, take the gloat, keep it remote

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. I had to take a moment just to sit and gloat.
2. Triumphant satisfaction, a gloat in the throat.
3. Watch 'em fall off while I raise the boat and gloat.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Triumphant victory lap
2. Enjoying a rival's failure
3. Lack of humility in success

## Tags:
attitude, common, unique, status

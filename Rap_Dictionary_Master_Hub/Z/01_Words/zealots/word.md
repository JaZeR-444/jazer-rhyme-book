# WORD: Zealots

## Meaning (plain):
Plural of zealot; people fanatically devoted to a cause; historically, a Jewish sect that militantly opposed Roman rule.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
A group of fanatical loyalists, the "army" for the cause, or a squad that won't compromise their beliefs.

## Syllables:
2 (zeal-ots)

## Part of speech:
Noun (Plural)

## Synonyms:
fanatics, radicals, partisans, activists

## Antonyms:
moderates, skeptics

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: plots, shots, pots / SLANT: ballots, mallets / MULTI: Zealots in the street, never know defeat, make the plan complete

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Surrounded by the Zealots, we holding the line.
2. Militant for the culture, we the modern Zealots.
3. No compromise, we moving like Zealots.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Historical militancy (opposition to "Roman" rule/authority)
2. Fanatical collective loyalty
3. Refusal to compromise or "sell out"

## Tags:
personality, history, collective, plural

# WORD: Zigged

## Meaning (plain):
Past tense of zig; having made a sharp turn or changed direction abruptly.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Already made the move that the competition missed; having outmaneuvered the opponent.

## Syllables:
1 (zigged)

## Part of speech:
Verb (Past Tense)

## Synonyms:
turned, dodged, shifted, swerved

## Antonyms:
zagged, stayed

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: bigged, digged, rigged / SLANT: wicked, thicket / MULTI: Zigged in the sight, out of the light, winning the fight

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. They tried to catch me, but I already Zigged.
2. The game was rigged, so I simply Zigged.
3. Left 'em in the dust when I abruptly Zigged.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Outmaneuvering/Dodging
2. Unpredictability in the past
3. "The game is rigged" (rhyme potential)

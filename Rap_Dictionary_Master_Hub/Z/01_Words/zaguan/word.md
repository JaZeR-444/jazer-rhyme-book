# WORD: Zaguan

## Meaning (plain):
A roofed passageway or vestibule leading from the entrance door of a house to a central courtyard, common in Spanish and Mexican architecture.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
The entrance to the "inner circle," the threshold of the home, or a transition space from the street to the sanctuary.

## Syllables:
2 (za-guan)

## Part of speech:
Noun

## Synonyms:
passageway, vestibule, entrance, hall

## Antonyms:
None

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: dawn, gone, lawn / SLANT: swan, baton / MULTI: Zaguan door, walking on the floor, wait for the encore

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Met her at the entrance, standing in the Zaguan.
2. From the street to the patio, pass through the Zaguan.
3. Shadows in the hall, waiting in the Zaguan.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Entrance to the "inner sanctum"
2. Transition from public (street) to private (courtyard)
3. Architectural luxury/culture

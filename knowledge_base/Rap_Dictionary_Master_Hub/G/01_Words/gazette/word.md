# WORD: Gazette

## Meaning (plain):
1. A newspaper (often used in names). 2. An official government journal containing lists of appointments, legal notices, and official announcements.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for achieving "official" status or having your name documented in the history of the game. When you're in the "gazette," your moves are official, recognized, and part of the public record.

## Syllables:
2 (ga-zette)

## Part of speech:
noun / verb

## Synonyms:
journal, record, newspaper, announcement, bulletin

## Antonyms:
rumor, secret, silence

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: net, jet, set / SLANT: regret, offset, sunset / MULTI: gazette name, part of the game, never the same

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Checked the gazette, my appointment is official.
2. Legal moves only, gazette with the signal.
3. Every win is documented, gazette in the journal.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Achieving "official" status
2. Public recognition/record of success
3. Listing "appointments" or promotions

## Tags:
media, law, status, unique

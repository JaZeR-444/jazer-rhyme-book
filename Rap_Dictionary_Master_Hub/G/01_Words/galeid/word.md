# WORD: Galeid

## Meaning (plain):
A member of the former shark family Galeidae (now mostly categorized under Carcharhinidae), which includes various ground sharks known for their predatory nature.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for a fierce, "shark-like" dominance in the water. Represents a predatory energy that is always on the hunt and dominates the "ground" level of the scene with lethal precision.

## Syllables:
2 (ga-leid)

## Part of speech:
noun

## Synonyms:
shark, predator, Carcharhinid, hunter

## Antonyms:
prey, weakling, victim

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: blade, paid, shade / SLANT: valid, solid, pallid / MULTI: galeid strike, end the night, lethal sight

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Moving through the water like a hungry galeid.
2. Predatory vision, keeping the galeid valid.
3. Lethal in the hunt, galeid in the front.

## 3 punchline angles:

2. Hunting the competition
3. "Ground shark" (street level) metaphors

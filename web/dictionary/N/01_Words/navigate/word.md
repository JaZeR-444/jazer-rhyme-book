# WORD: Navigate

## Meaning (plain):
To plan and direct the course of a ship, aircraft, or other transport; to find one's way through a complex area or situation.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Metaphor for maneuvering through the industry and the streets. Represents the skill of finding the right path to success while avoiding the "icebergs" and hazards of the game. A master navigator knows how to steer the career through any storm.

## Syllables:
3 (nav-i-gate)

## Part of speech:
verb

## Synonyms:
steer, pilot, maneuver, guide, direct, find-way

## Antonyms:
get-lost, drift, wander, crash

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: state, great, fate / SLANT: update, dictate, vibrate / MULTI: navigate the maze, through the haze, watch the blaze

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. I had to navigate the industry to find the throne.
2. Steering through the hazards, navigate alone.
3. Every move is calculated, navigate the zone.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Maneuvering through street/industry hazards
2. "Steering" the career to success
3. Finding a way through a complex "maze"

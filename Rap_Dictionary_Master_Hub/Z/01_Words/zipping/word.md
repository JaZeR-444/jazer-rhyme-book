# WORD: Zipping

## Meaning (plain):
Present participle of zip; fastening with a zipper; moving very fast; or compressing files into a sequence.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Actively moving through the competition at high speed; "fastening" up the deal while everyone else is still talking.

## Syllables:
2 (zip-ping)

## Part of speech:
Verb (Present Participle)

## Synonyms:
speeding, flying, fastening, compressing

## Antonyms:
unzipping, slowing

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: flipping, tripping, dipping, ripping / SLANT: listing, twisting / MULTI: Zipping through the rain, washing out the pain, reaching for the gain

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Zipping through the lane, they can't catch the ghost.
2. I'm actively Zipping up the case, I'm the host.
3. Fast movement, yeah I'm Zipping by.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. "High Speed" (Continuous movement)
2. "Fastening/Closing" (Active deal-making)
3. "Zipping/Tripping" (Speed vs Stumbling)

## Tags:
movement, action, speed, energy

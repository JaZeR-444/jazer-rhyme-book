# WORD: Zambo

## Meaning (plain):
A historical racial term in the Americas for people of mixed African and Indigenous ancestry; part of the colonial "casta" system.

## Rap meaning (how I'd say it):
Representing a complex, powerful mix of lineages, the intersection of African and Indigenous struggles, and reclaiming historical identity.

## Syllables:
2 (zam-bo)

## Part of speech:
Noun

## Synonyms:
mixed-race, Cafuzo (Brazil), Lobos (Mexico)

## Antonyms:
None

## Rhyme ideas (end / slant / multi):
END: combo, mambo, jumbo / SLANT: shadow, hollow / MULTI: Zambo blood, through the fire and flood, roots in the mud

## 3 bar-ready examples:
1. Mixed roots, powerful like a Zambo.
2. Two worlds in my veins, call it the Zambo.
3. History on my back, the spirit of a Zambo.

## 3 punchline angles:
1. Intersection of African and Indigenous heritage
2. Reclaiming identity from colonial "casta" systems
3. Global/Diaspora unity
